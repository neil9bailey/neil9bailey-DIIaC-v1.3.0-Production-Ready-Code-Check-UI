# External Regulatory Source Notes (captured 2026-03-10)

## EU AI Act

- EU AI Act portal and timeline:
  - https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/policies/regulatory-framework-ai
- Official legal text PDF (OJ L 2024/1689):
  - https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/PDF/?uri=OJ:L_202401689

Key observations used in overview:

- Timeline statements include first obligations from February 2, 2025 and broader application phases through August 2, 2026 / 2027.
- Fine tiers in Article 99 include amounts up to EUR 35,000,000 or 7% turnover, EUR 15,000,000 or 3%, and EUR 7,500,000 or 1%.

## EU GDPR

- Official GDPR legal text PDF (CELEX:32016R0679):
  - https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/PDF/?uri=CELEX:32016R0679

Key observation used in overview:

- Article 83 fine tiers include up to EUR 20,000,000 or 4% (higher tier) and EUR 10,000,000 or 2% (lower tier).

## UK Data Protection / AI Regulation Position

- ICO UK GDPR fines overview:
  - https://ico.org.uk/for-organisations/uk-gdpr-guidance-and-resources/introduction-to-data-protection/guide-to-the-general-data-protection-regulation-gdpr/enforcement-and-penalties/
- UK government response (pro-innovation AI regulation approach):
  - https://www.gov.uk/government/consultations/ai-regulation-a-pro-innovation-approach/outcome/a-pro-innovation-approach-to-ai-regulation-government-response
- UK Parliament AI Regulation Bill [HL] page:
  - https://bills.parliament.uk/bills/3513

Key observations used in overview:

- ICO public guidance states UK GDPR fine tiers up to GBP 17.5m/4% and GBP 8.7m/2%.
- UK government response describes a principles-based, non-statutory approach through existing regulators.
- Parliament bill page indicates an AI Regulation Bill exists in process, but not identified as enacted law in these reviewed sources.
